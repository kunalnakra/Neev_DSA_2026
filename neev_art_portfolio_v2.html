About Me

My name is Neev Nakra and I am in Primary 6. I have loved drawing for as long as I can remember. I draw because I enjoy it — not because of school or any class. I like trying different styles. Sometimes I draw detailed pencil illustrations of machines and robots, where I try to get every joint and panel right. Other times I paint in a more abstract way, using shapes and colours to express an idea rather than draw something realistic. I find that art and the other things I like — reading, robotics, building things — all come from the same place: wanting to make something that did not exist before.

Work 01 / 02

Totems of Africa

Acrylic painting  ·  2025

Abstract Painting Cubist style

My thoughts on this piece

I was interested in African totem art and wanted to paint something inspired by it. I decided to break the shapes up into geometric pieces — triangles, rectangles and curved forms — instead of drawing things realistically.

If you look closely, some of the shapes in the trees look a bit like faces. That was intentional. I wanted to give the painting a feeling that there are hidden things in nature if you look carefully enough.

I chose earthy colours — browns, oranges and greens — because they felt right for the subject.

Work 02 / 02

Construction Robot

Pencil on paper  ·  2025

Pencil Illustration Original design

My thoughts on this piece

I designed this robot from scratch — I did not copy it from anywhere. I imagined a robot that would work on a construction site, which is why I drew cranes and machines in the background to show how big it is.

I spent a lot of time on the joints and panels to make the robot look like it could actually move and work. I am also interested in robotics, so I think about how machines are built, which helped me draw this.

The container it is holding in one hand gives it a sense of scale — something that a normal person could not lift at all.
